# Deal Structures: How Artists Make and Lose Money

A reference comparison of the primary deal structures in the US music industry.

---

## Deal Type Comparison

| Feature | Traditional Major Deal | 360 Deal | Indie Label Deal | Self-Distribution |
|---------|----------------------|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Advance** | $50K–$2M+ (varies widely) | Often larger (more revenue streams pledged) | $0–$50K typically | None |
| **Royalty rate** | 15–20% | 15–20% on recordings | ~50% | 85–100% |
| **Non-recording revenue** | Not included | 10–35% of touring, merch, endorsements, publishing | Not typically included | Not applicable |
| **Master ownership** | Label owns (life of copyright) | Label owns | Varies (often reverts after term) | Artist owns |
| **Recoupment** | From artist's royalty share | From all revenue streams (cross-collateralized) | From artist's share | N/A |
| **Marketing/promo** | Label-funded (recoupable) | Label-funded (recoupable) | Limited | Artist-funded |
| **Distribution** | Label-owned | Label-owned | Via major or indie distributor | Via aggregator |
| **Creative control** | Label has significant input | Label has significant input | More artist autonomy | Full artist control |
| **Term** | Multi-album (3–7 albums typical) | Multi-album | Varies | Per-release |

## How Recoupment Works: A Worked Example

**Scenario**: Artist signs a traditional major label deal.

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Advance | $500,000 |
| Recording costs | $200,000 |
| Music video | $150,000 |
| Marketing/promotion | $100,000 |
| **Total recoupable costs** | **$950,000** |

**Artist's royalty rate**: 18% of revenue

For the label to recoup $950,000 from the artist's 18% share:
- Total revenue needed: $950,000 ÷ 0.18 = **$5,278,000**

At streaming rates (~$0.004 per stream average):
- Revenue per stream to rights holder (~70% of gross): $0.0028
- Streams needed to generate $5.28M to rights holders: ~**1.89 billion streams**
- Of which the artist's 18% = $950K (recoupment point)

**Until the label recoups $950K from the artist's share, the artist earns $0 in royalties** — even though the label has been collecting the other 82% of revenue from day one.

After recoupment, the artist earns 18% of ongoing revenue. The label continues earning 82%.

**Note**: The label keeps 100% of its share (82%) from the first dollar. Recoupment only applies to the artist's share. This is the fundamental asymmetry of the major label deal.

## Historical Deductions (Pre-Digital)

These deductions reduced the base price before royalties were calculated:

| Deduction | Typical Rate | Rationale | Still Applied? |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Packaging deduction** | 20–25% off retail price | Cover CD/vinyl packaging costs | Rare in new digital deals; may persist in legacy contracts |
| **Breakage deduction** | 10% off units | Originally for shellac records breaking in transit | Eliminated in most modern contracts |
| **Free goods** | 10–15% of units | Promotional copies given to retailers | Eliminated in digital era |
| **New technology deduction** | Up to 20% | Reduced rate for "new" formats (CDs, initially) | No |

**Combined effect**: An artist with a headline 18% royalty rate could see their effective rate reduced to **10–12%** after all deductions.

*Sources: Sound On Sound, HowStuffWorks, Songstuff*

## Revenue Flow: Major Label Artist vs. Indie Artist

### Major Label Artist (18% royalty, post-recoupment)

```
$9.99 subscriber payment
    ├── Platform keeps: $3.00 (30%)
    └── To rights holders: $6.99 (70%)
         ├── Label keeps: $5.73 (82% of $6.99)
         └── Artist receives: $1.26 (18% of $6.99)
```

**Per-stream (Spotify avg ~$0.004):**
- Platform: ~$0.0012
- Label: ~$0.0023
- Artist: ~$0.0005

### Independent Artist (via DistroKid)

```
$9.99 subscriber payment
    ├── Platform keeps: $3.00 (30%)
    └── To rights holders: $6.99 (70%)
         └── Artist receives: $6.99 (100%, minus flat annual fee)
```

**Per-stream (Spotify avg ~$0.004):**
- Platform: ~$0.0012
- Artist: ~$0.0028

**The indie artist earns roughly 5.6x more per stream than the major label artist** — but without the marketing, playlist placement, and promotional infrastructure the major provides.

## Publishing Revenue Flows

### The Two Copyrights

| Copyright | Who Owns It | Revenue Types |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Composition** (publishing) | Songwriter / publisher | Mechanical, performance, sync |
| **Sound recording** (master) | Label or artist | Streaming, sales, sync |

### Mechanical Royalty Rate (US)

- **Physical/downloads**: $0.127 per unit (as of Jan 1, 2025, for songs under 5 minutes)
- **Streaming**: Calculated as a percentage of service revenue (administered by the MLC)

### Performance Royalties (PRO Split)

```
PRO collects from broadcasters/venues/streaming
    ├── 50% → Songwriter
    └── 50% → Publisher
```

### Sync Licensing
- Negotiated case by case
- Requires licenses from **both** the composition owner (publisher) and the recording owner (label/artist)
- Can range from a few hundred dollars (student film) to millions (Super Bowl ad)
