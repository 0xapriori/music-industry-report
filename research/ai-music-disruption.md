# AI Disruption in the Music Industry

**Research compiled: March 2026**
**Methodology:** Web-sourced from industry publications, news outlets, legal databases, and platform announcements. All statistics are attributed. Items marked [single-source] have not been independently corroborated across multiple outlets.

## 1. AI Music Generation Tools — Current State (March 2026)

### 1.1 Suno

**Funding and Valuation:**
- Closed a $250M Series C round at a $2.45B post-money valuation (November 2025). Round led by Menlo Ventures with participation from NVentures (NVIDIA's venture arm), Hallwood Media, Lightspeed, and Matrix. ([Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/music/music-industry-news/ai-music-suno-secures-250-million-funding-round-1236431144/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/11/19/legally-embattled-ai-music-startup-suno-raises-at-2-45b-valuation-on-200m-revenue/))
- Reported $200M ARR at the time of the Series C (November 2025); Suno claimed $300M ARR and 2M paid subscribers as of February 2026. ([Music Business Worldwide](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/suno-hits-2m-paid-subscribers-300m-annual-revenue/), [Digital Music News](https://www.digitalmusicnews.com/2026/02/26/suno-subscribers-february-2026/))

**Legal Settlements and Licensed Model:**
- Settled with Warner Music Group in November 2025. The settlement resolves copyright litigation and establishes a licensing partnership. Under the deal, Suno will launch new, more advanced models in 2026 trained on licensed music; current (unlicensed) models will be deprecated. ([Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/suno-warner-music-group-ai-music-settlement-lawsuit-1235472868/), [Music Ally](https://musically.com/2025/11/25/ai-music-firm-suno-strikes-first-licensing-deal-with-warner-music-group/))
- UMG's lawsuit against Suno remains ongoing as of March 2026. Sony Music's lawsuit also remains unresolved. ([Music Business Worldwide](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/universal-and-suno-are-in-a-pr-battle-over-walled-gardens-in-ai-music/))

**Platform Capabilities:**
- Generates complete songs (vocals, instrumentals, lyrics) from text prompts using proprietary v5 models, up to 8 minutes in length.
- Produces 90+ second songs in under 60 seconds.
- Quality sweet spot: short-form, vocal-driven content requiring minimal post-production.
- Known limitations: inconsistency in following prompts, mispronounced words, repeated lyrics, and poor stem separation (bleed between tracks makes professional mixing difficult). ([Suno review roundups](https://aisongcreator.pro/blog/suno-ai-review))

**Usage Statistics:**
- Over 100 million people worldwide have used Suno since launch. [single-source: Unite.AI / Business of Apps]
- 2.5 million monthly active users; 2 million paid subscribers as of February 2026.
- Users generate approximately 7 million tracks per day — surpassing Spotify's entire ~100M-song catalog approximately every 14 days. [single-source: EDM.com, citing Suno data]
- Downloaded close to 30 million times, peaking in Q2 2025. [single-source: Business of Apps]

**Monetization Changes:**
- Free-tier users can no longer download songs (songs are playable and shareable only). Downloading requires a paid subscription.
- Paid subscribers retain commercial use rights for music generated while subscription is active, including streaming, downloads, and sync licensing.

### 1.2 Udio

**Legal Settlements and Licensed Platform:**
- Built by ex-Google DeepMind engineers; launched April 2024.
- Settled with UMG in October 2025 — the first strategic agreement of its kind. Under the deal, UMG artists and songwriters can participate on an opt-in basis in a new licensed AI music creation platform launching in 2026. ([PR Newswire / UMG](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/universal-music-group-and-udio-announce-udios-first-strategic-agreements-for-new-licensed-ai-music-creation-platform-302599129.html), [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/pro/ai-music-udio-settles-lawsuit-universal-music-group/))
- Settled with WMG in November 2025. Similar structure: licensing deal for a "next-generation" AI music creation/listening/discovery platform in 2026. ([Music Business Worldwide](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/warner-music-settles-udio-lawsuit-strikes-licensing-deal-with-ai-platform/))
- Sony Music's lawsuit against Udio remains ongoing.
- Under the settlement terms, Udio halted external song downloads. Users are locked into the Udio ecosystem — songs cannot be shared outside the platform. ([WebProNews](https://www.webpronews.com/udio-halts-ai-song-downloads-after-copyright-settlement-with-umg-warner/))

**Platform Capabilities:**
- Prioritizes audio fidelity and instrumental quality over vocal generation.
- Industry experts rate Udio output as "almost indistinguishable from" real recordings for instrumental and production quality.
- Takes 90+ seconds to generate tracks of similar length to Suno's sub-60-second output.
- The planned 2026 subscription service will enable mashups, remixes, tempo changes, and voice swapping with participating artists' voices (opt-in).

**Revenue:** Specific ARR figures for Udio have not been publicly disclosed. No competitor has reported a paying subscriber base at Suno's scale.

### 1.3 Other Major Platforms

**Stable Audio (Stability AI):**
- Now at version 2.5. Enterprise-grade sound production tool trained on fully licensed datasets.
- Commercially safe models designed for professional use cases.
- Available via StableAudio.com, the Stability AI API, or partner platforms. Enterprise licenses allow on-premises deployment and fine-tuning on organizational sound libraries. ([Stability AI](https://stability.ai/stable-audio))

**AIVA (Artificial Intelligence Virtual Artist):**
- Specializes in orchestral and cinematic music; offers 250+ styles.
- Widely used by film composers. Orchestral output is considered competitive with human compositions by multiple reviewers.
- Provides MIDI export for full compositional control — a key differentiator allowing human post-production editing at the note level.

**Boomy:**
- Has facilitated creation of over 19 million songs to date.
- Approximately 14,800 new songs created daily on the platform. [single-source: Boomy / Music Ally]
- Unique distribution feature: direct upload to 40+ streaming and social platforms with monetization.
- In May 2023, Spotify removed approximately 7% of Boomy tracks due to detected stream manipulation (bot activity). Boomy denied involvement; uploads resumed within days. ([Music Business Worldwide](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/ai-music-app-boomy-spotify-stream-manipulation/), [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/pro/spotify-removed-thousands-songs-fight-streaming-fraud-ai/))
- Subsequently signed a distribution deal with Warner Music Group's Alternative Distribution Alliance (ADA) in November 2023 for select artists.

**Soundful:**
- Emphasizes royalty-free creation for commercial use.
- Algorithms trained note-by-note alongside industry producers and sound engineers.
- Claims its AI will never replicate an existing song — designed for original, commercially safe output. [single-source: Soundful marketing materials]

### 1.4 Quality Benchmarks: AI vs. Human Music

**Formal Studies:**
- A large-scale study generated 6,000 songs across 12 models and ran 15,600 pairwise comparisons with 2,500+ participants, producing Elo ratings for preference and text-audio alignment. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.19085v1)) [single-source]
- The "Echoes of Humanity" study used randomized controlled crossover design: when song pairs were random, listeners could not reliably distinguish AI from human music (accuracy matched random guessing). When pairs were deliberately similar, discrimination improved. Vocals were the primary "tell" for detecting AI origin. [single-source]

**Qualitative Findings:**
- AI music scores high in curiosity, fascination, and relaxation categories; human music prevails in empathy, nostalgia, and intensity.
- AI excels at complex harmonies and rhythmic balance but struggles with unpredictability — the slight imperfections and expressive timing that create what psychologists call the "human imperfection effect."
- Structured listening sessions using Mean Opinion Score (MOS) ratings and A/B comparisons remain the gold standard for evaluation.

**Practical Assessment:**
- For background/functional music (ads, content creation, ambient): AI-generated music is already competitive with human production.
- For emotionally complex, culturally specific, or performance-driven music: human producers and performers retain a clear advantage, particularly in vocal expression, lyrical nuance, and genre authenticity.

## 2. AI in Music Production (Non-Generative)

### 2.1 AI Mastering

**LANDR:**
- One of the original AI mastering services. Its Synapse mastering engine analyzes audio features and custom-builds a mastering chain for each track.
- Also offers LANDR Stems plugin for stem separation, powered by AudioShake's AI algorithms (used by major labels and publishers). ([LANDR Blog](https://blog.landr.com/ai-in-music/))

**iZotope:**
- iZotope RX is the industry standard for audio repair and processing.
- AI-powered Music Rebalance and stem separation used in professional studios worldwide.
- Goes beyond separation: noise reduction, de-reverb, de-click, and spectral editing.

**CloudBounce:**
- Automated mastering service; less prominently covered in 2025-2026 industry reporting than LANDR or iZotope. Specific recent usage statistics not available.

### 2.2 Stem Separation

**LALAL.AI:**
- The most popular dedicated AI vocal isolation and stem separation service.
- Latest engine separates into up to 10 categories: vocals, drums, bass, guitar, piano, strings, wind instruments, and more.
- Handles complex mixes with minimal artifacts. ([Slooply](https://slooply.com/blog/top-10-ai-tools-for-music-producers-2025-the-ultimate-guide/))

**Demucs (Meta):**
- Open-source, free. Requires Python or community-built interfaces.
- Latest Hybrid Transformer model particularly strong on vocals and drums.
- Quality rivals paid services for those comfortable with technical setup. ([MusicRadar](https://www.musicradar.com/music-tech/i-tested-11-of-the-best-stem-separation-tools-and-you-might-already-have-the-winner-in-your-daw))

### 2.3 How Producers Actually Use AI in Workflows

Typical workflow integration as of 2025-2026:
1. Upload tracks to LALAL.AI or Moises for stem separation
2. Download isolated vocals/instrumentals into DAW
3. Build remixes or new arrangements from separated elements
4. Use LANDR or iZotope Ozone for automated mastering

**Adoption rate:** One widely cited figure claims over 60% of musicians now incorporate AI tools for composition and editing. However, the sourcing on this statistic is unclear and should be treated with caution. [single-source, unverified methodology]

**Production efficiency:** Internal data from major agencies reportedly shows time-to-final-cut for commercial music can fall by 50-70% with AI tools. [single-source: Rolling Stone Culture Council, citing unnamed agency data]

### 2.4 Voice Synthesis and Cloning

- AI voice cloning technology can now produce realistic singing voices that closely mimic specific artists.
- Multiple platforms offer voice cloning capabilities, with varying degrees of quality and ethical safeguards.
- The Udio/UMG 2026 platform will include voice swapping with participating artists' voices on an opt-in basis — representing the first major-label-sanctioned use of commercial voice cloning.
- See Section 5 for unauthorized voice cloning concerns and the Drake deepfake incidents.

## 3. AI and Streaming Platforms

### 3.1 Scale of AI-Generated Music on Streaming Platforms

- Suno users alone generate approximately 7 million tracks per day.
- Boomy has facilitated creation of 19+ million songs total, with ~14,800 new songs daily.
- Approximately 100,000 new commercial tracks are uploaded to streaming services daily. AI-generated music constitutes a growing share of this volume, though the exact proportion is difficult to isolate because not all AI-generated content is labeled as such.
- In the past 12 months (as of September 2025), Spotify removed more than 75 million spam tracks, many of which were AI-generated or AI-facilitated. ([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2025-09-25/spotify-strengthens-ai-protections/))

### 3.2 Spotify's AI Policies

**September 2025 policy update — three pillars:**
1. Protecting artists from impersonation and deepfakes
2. Combating AI-driven spam and royalty fraud
3. Ensuring transparency about when and how AI is used in music

**Detection patterns Spotify monitors:**
- Mass uploads from the same account
- Excessive duplicates with slightly different metadata
- Intentional SEO manipulation and metadata hacking
- Artificially short tracks (just over 30 seconds to qualify for royalty-bearing streams)

**2026 compliance requirements:**
- AI-generated music is allowed only if creators verify compliance with IP and ethical sourcing standards.
- Creators must disclose whether training data included copyrighted audio and confirm all rights-holders provided consent.

([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2025-09-25/spotify-strengthens-ai-protections/), [iMusician](https://imusician.pro/en/resources/blog/spotify-ai-policy-in-2025-artist-protection-transparency), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/08/nx-s1-5492314/ai-music-streaming-services-spotify))

### 3.3 YouTube's AI Music Policies

- As of October 2025, YouTube requires creators to disclose AI-generated content in metadata and visual indicators.
- Fully AI-generated music (audio-only, unmodified) is ineligible for monetization and Content ID as of July 2025.
- Transformative human input (vocals, lyrics, detailed edits, commentary) is required to qualify for monetization, though approval is not guaranteed.
- YouTube is building systems to reduce low-quality AI content, citing repetitive AI patterns, shared AI datasets, and near-identical melodies as rejection criteria. ([YouTube policy updates via LastPlayDistro](https://lastplaydistro.com/blog/youtube-content-id-ai-generated-music-policy-2026-what-creators-must-know), [iMusician](https://imusician.pro/en/resources/blog/youtube-to-prioritize-managing-ai-slop-in-2026))

### 3.4 The Michael Smith Fraud Case

- Michael Smith, 52, of Cornelius, North Carolina, was charged by the U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of New York in September 2024.
- Used AI to generate thousands of fake songs with fabricated titles (e.g., "Zygotic Washstands," "Zymotechnical") by fake acts (e.g., "Callous Post," "Calorie Screams").
- Created thousands of bot accounts on Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music to stream songs continuously — approximately 661,000 streams per day.
- Generated over $10 million in fraudulent royalties between 2017 and 2024, with annual royalties of approximately $1.2 million.
- Faces fraud and conspiracy charges carrying a potential penalty of up to 60 years in prison.
- Pleaded not guilty. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2024/09/05/ai-artificial-intelligence-music-fraud-conspiracy-michael-smith-royalties/), [Variety](https://variety.com/2024/music/news/ai-songs-spotify-apple-michael-smith-fraud-charged-artificial-intelligence-1236130680/), [Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/streaming-fraud-fake-streams-mike-smith-1235500686/))

### 3.5 How AI Dilutes the Royalty Pool

Streaming platforms like Spotify use a pro-rata royalty model: total subscription revenue is divided among all streams. Every additional stream — including those from AI-generated content — reduces the per-stream payout for all artists. As AI-generated tracks multiply:

- The denominator (total streams) grows faster than the numerator (total revenue), reducing per-stream rates.
- Human artists compete for listener attention against an exponentially growing catalog of AI-generated content.
- Spotify's 75 million spam track removals in 2024-2025 suggest the scale of the problem, though not all spam tracks are AI-generated.

Exact figures on royalty dilution attributable specifically to AI-generated music are not publicly available. The effect is real but its precise magnitude remains unquantified.

## 4. Positive Externalities of AI in Music

### 4.1 Democratization of Production

- AI tools give independent artists access to production capabilities previously available only to those with significant resources or major label backing.
- Anyone with a laptop can now access rich sonic palettes that once required expensive hardware or years of training.
- Professional-quality mastering, mixing, and stem separation are available at a fraction of traditional studio costs.
- Indie artists can produce, market, and release music at a professional level without major label support. ([RouteNote](https://routenote.com/blog/ai-in-the-music-industry-how-artists-can-use-ai-in-2025/))

### 4.2 Accessibility

- AI music creation tools lower barriers for musicians with physical disabilities who may face challenges with traditional instruments or studio equipment.
- Text-to-music interfaces enable music creation through description rather than physical performance.
- Specific usage data on AI music tool adoption by disabled musicians is not publicly available.

### 4.3 New Creative Possibilities

- AI as a collaborative tool: producers use AI for idea generation, arrangement suggestions, and exploring genre combinations.
- "BBL Drizzy" — created by comedian King Willonius using Udio — became the first AI-generated song to receive a sample clearance after producer Metro Boomin remixed it. This represents a new model of AI-human creative collaboration. ([Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/lists/biggest-ai-music-stories-2024-drake-tiktok-lawsuits/drake-deepfakes-tupacs-voice))
- The Udio/UMG 2026 platform will enable mashups, remixes, tempo changes, and voice swapping with consenting artists — opening new creative avenues within a licensed framework.

### 4.4 Reduction in Production Costs

- Time-to-final-cut for commercial music reportedly reduced by 50-70% with AI tools. [single-source: unnamed agency data via Rolling Stone Culture Council]
- AI mastering services (LANDR, CloudBounce) cost a fraction of professional studio mastering sessions.
- Stem separation tools eliminate the need for multi-track recordings in many remix and sampling workflows.

### 4.5 Personalized/Adaptive Music Experiences

- **Spotify AI DJ:** Now accepts voice and text requests, using neural text-to-speech for naturalistic commentary. Leverages listener history for personalized music selection. ([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2023-02-22/spotify-debuts-a-new-ai-dj-right-in-your-pocket/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/05/13/spotifys-ai-dj-now-lets-you-use-voice-commands-to-personalize-your-tunes/))
- **Spotify Prompted Playlists:** Users describe desired listening experience in natural language; AI generates playlists informed by listening history. Beta expanded to 40+ markets in 2025; further expanded in January 2026. ([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2026-01-22/prompted-playlists-expansion/))
- **Smart Reorder:** Automatically reorganizes playlist tracks by tempo (BPM), key, and energy levels to mimic professional DJ transitions. ([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2026-02-25/smart-reorder-playlist-mixing/))

## 5. Negative Externalities of AI in Music

### 5.1 Job Displacement

- Automation directly threatens session musicians, jingle writers, stock library composers, and composers for low-budget projects.
- Automated mixing, mastering, scoring, and production systems reduce opportunities for audio engineers, producers, and session musicians.
- New roles are emerging (prompt engineering, AI curation, dataset curation, AI model tuning, sample library creation, soundtrack customization), but the redistribution of creative labor is uneven. ([Rolling Stone Culture Council](https://council.rollingstone.com/blog/the-impacts-and-disruption-of-ai-on-music-industry-stakeholders/))
- Specific job loss statistics for the music industry are not publicly available. The impact is most acute in commercial/functional music (ads, background music, corporate content) rather than in artist-driven recordings.

### 5.2 Royalty Dilution

- See Section 3.5. The pro-rata streaming model means every additional AI-generated stream reduces per-stream payouts for all artists.
- Suno's 7 million tracks/day output alone represents a massive expansion of the global music catalog.
- The Michael Smith case ($10M in fraudulent royalties) demonstrates the vulnerability of the royalty pool to AI-enabled exploitation.

### 5.3 Deepfakes and Unauthorized Voice Cloning

**"Heart on My Sleeve" (April 2023):**
- AI-generated track featuring synthetic voices of Drake and The Weeknd went viral (20+ million views/streams) before UMG issued a DMCA takedown. ([Music Business Research](https://musicbusinessresearch.wordpress.com/2024/05/27/ai-in-the-music-industry-part-17-fake-drake-and-the-problem-of-deep-fakes/))

**Drake's Use of AI Tupac Voice (2024):**
- Drake used AI to replicate Tupac Shakur's voice on a Kendrick Lamar diss track. The Shakur estate sent a cease and desist letter, calling it a "blatant abuse" of Shakur's legacy. ([Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/lists/biggest-ai-music-stories-2024-drake-tiktok-lawsuits/drake-deepfakes-tupacs-voice))

**Broader Concerns:**
- Voice cloning technology is increasingly accessible and affordable.
- Unauthorized clones of artists' voices can be used for commercial gain, political messaging, or reputational damage.
- The technology creates fundamental questions about identity, consent, and artistic integrity.

### 5.4 Devaluation of Human Creativity

- As AI-generated music becomes indistinguishable from human music in blind tests (for random pairings), the perceived value of human musical creativity faces pressure.
- The "human imperfection effect" — expressive timing, subtle imperfections — may become a premium differentiator rather than a default expectation.
- Bloomberg opinion has argued AI could paradoxically make human-performed genres like jazz more popular by emphasizing what AI cannot replicate. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2025-12-26/why-ai-could-make-human-jazz-popular-again)) [single-source]

### 5.5 Concentration of Power

- AI music generation companies (Suno at $2.45B valuation, backed by NVIDIA) represent potential new gatekeepers in the music ecosystem.
- The licensing deals between AI companies and major labels may entrench the existing power structure: major labels control the training data, AI companies control the generation technology, and independent artists and smaller labels are marginalized.
- Labels may receive equity stakes in AI companies as part of settlements, further concentrating economic power. [single-source: Music Business Worldwide, referring to likely settlement structures]

### 5.6 Training Data Consent Issues

- The original lawsuits against Suno and Udio alleged mass unauthorized use of copyrighted recordings for model training.
- Independent musicians have also filed complaints (e.g., country artist Anthony Justice). ([Copyright Alliance](https://copyrightalliance.org/ai-copyright-lawsuit-developments-2025/))
- The settlement model emerging (UMG/Udio, WMG/Suno) requires future models to be trained on licensed/authorized music — but this does not retroactively address training that already occurred.
- Major labels have refused to commit to securing individual creator consent for basic AI training, reportedly believing they control catalog copyrights sufficiently to authorize training themselves. This creates a tension between label rights and individual artist rights. ([Complete Music Update](https://completemusicupdate.com/music-and-ai-2025s-developments-that-will-shape-2026s-disputes/))
- UMG's deal with Udio is structured as opt-in for artists — a stronger consent model than opt-out alternatives.

### 5.7 Loss of Cultural Authenticity

- AI models trained predominantly on Western popular music may homogenize global musical output.
- Inclusivity and democratization frequently function as marketing rhetoric rather than genuine guiding principles for AI music companies. ([arxiv.org, "Opening Musical Creativity?"](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.08805v1)) [single-source: academic paper]
- The ability to generate music in any style without cultural context or lived experience raises questions about appropriation and authenticity.
- Specific data on cultural impact is limited; this remains a primarily qualitative concern.

## 6. Industry Responses

### 6.1 Major Label Strategies

**Universal Music Group (UMG):**
- Settled with Udio (October 2025); partnering on licensed AI platform launching 2026.
- Lawsuit against Suno remains ongoing.
- Opt-in model for artists — artists and songwriters choose whether to participate.
- Announced a deal with Spotify to produce "responsible AI products." [single-source: Hollywood Reporter]

**Warner Music Group (WMG):**
- Settled with both Suno and Udio (November 2025).
- Licensing deals span recorded music and music publishing businesses.
- Partnered with Boomy's ADA distribution deal (November 2023).
- Strategy: controlled cooperation rather than blanket opposition to AI.

**Sony Music:**
- Has not settled with either Suno or Udio; lawsuits remain ongoing as of March 2026.
- Maintains a more adversarial litigation stance compared to UMG and WMG.

**Overall Strategic Direction:**
- The major labels are shifting from pure litigation to "controlled cooperation" — using settlements to shape the AI music ecosystem rather than attempting to prevent it.
- Settlements likely include equity stakes and revenue-sharing arrangements, though specific financial terms are confidential.
- The emerging model gives labels significant influence over how AI music tools develop, what training data is used, and how revenue is distributed.

### 6.2 Artist Responses

**Drake AI Deepfake Incidents:**
- The "Heart on My Sleeve" deepfake (April 2023) became a catalyst for industry-wide discussion about AI voice cloning. UMG removed it via DMCA.
- Drake's own use of AI-generated Tupac voice in 2024 highlighted the complexity: artists can be both victims and perpetrators of AI voice cloning.

**Human Artistry Campaign:**
- SAG-AFTRA supports the Human Artistry Campaign's core principles for AI applications in support of human creativity.
- The campaign advocates for consent, credit, and compensation when AI interacts with human artistry.

**"BBL Drizzy":**
- The first AI-generated song to receive a sample clearance, demonstrating a path for AI-human creative collaboration within existing legal frameworks.

### 6.3 Legislative Responses

**ELVIS Act (Tennessee, 2024):**
- First enacted U.S. legislation specifically designed to protect musicians from unauthorized AI use of their voices.
- Signed March 21, 2024; effective July 1, 2024.
- Explicitly includes a person's voice (actual and simulated) as a protected property right for the first time.
- Exploitation of digital likenesses without explicit permission can be legally challenged by the individual or their estate. ([American Bar Association](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/litigation/resources/newsletters/intellectual-property/elvis-act-new-law-addresses-impact-ai-music-industry/), [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/business/legal/tennessee-elvis-act-protecting-artists-voices-ai-impersonation-1235637934/))

**NO FAKES Act (Federal, pending):**
- Would prevent unauthorized digital replicas of individuals' names, faces, and voices.
- Gained bipartisan support by end of 2024; reintroduced in April 2025.
- Backed by the Recording Academy and music rights-holder organizations. ([Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2024/04/first-of-its-kind-ai-law-addresses-deep-fakes-and-voice-clones), [Temple 10-Q](https://law.temple.edu/10q/the-clone-wars-a-new-congress-reconsiders-the-no-fakes-act-to-combat-digital-deepfakes/))
- Status as of March 2026: not yet enacted into law. Specific current legislative status not confirmed in available sources.

**EU AI Act:**
- Came into force August 1, 2024; most provisions apply as of August 2, 2026.
- AI music generation tools classified as "limited risk" with additional transparency obligations.
- Requires: artist consent before voices/styles/compositions are used in training; transparency about training data; fair compensation for rights-holders.
- Creative industry groups have argued the EU AI Act does not go far enough to protect artists' copyright. ([Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2025/08/02/eus-ai-act-doesnt-do-enough-to-protect-artists-copyright-creative-groups-say))
- In January 2026, the European Parliament committee advanced additional AI proposals hailed by music industry bodies. ([Music Ally](https://musically.com/2026/01/29/music-bodies-hail-european-parliament-committee-ai-proposals/))

### 6.4 Union Responses

**SAG-AFTRA:**
- Ratified the Sound Recording Code in April 2024 with major labels (UMG, Sony, WMG, Disney Music Group). Agreement spans January 1, 2021 through December 31, 2026.
- Key AI provisions:
  - Clear and conspicuous consent required before releasing recordings using digital replication of an artist's voice.
  - Minimum compensation requirements.
  - Specific details of intended use must be provided prior to release.
  - The terms "artist," "singer," and "royalty artist" are defined to include humans only.
- SAG-AFTRA's position: digital simulation of performers and reuse of recorded performances are mandatory subjects of bargaining under the National Labor Relations Act.
([SAG-AFTRA](https://www.sagaftra.org/contracts-industry-resources/member-resources/artificial-intelligence), [US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/top-news/articles/2024-04-12/sag-aftra-union-secures-ai-protections-for-artists-in-deal-with-major-record-labels), [Music Ally](https://musically.com/2024/04/15/sag-aftras-tentative-new-labels-deal-includes-ai-provisions/))

### 6.5 Platform Responses

**Spotify:**
- Removed 75+ million spam tracks in 12 months (as of September 2025).
- Requires AI-generated music disclosure and IP compliance verification (2026).
- Announced deals with UMG, Sony, and WMG to produce "responsible AI products."
- Launched AI DJ, Prompted Playlists, and Smart Reorder features that use AI to enhance (not replace) the listening experience.

**YouTube:**
- AI-generated content disclosure required in metadata and visual indicators (October 2025).
- Fully AI-generated audio-only music ineligible for monetization and Content ID (July 2025).
- Human input (vocals, lyrics, edits, commentary) required for monetization eligibility.
- Prioritizing "managing AI slop" in 2026.

**Apple Music and SoundCloud:**
- Have introduced similar AI content frameworks to Spotify's, though specific policy details are less extensively documented in available sources.
