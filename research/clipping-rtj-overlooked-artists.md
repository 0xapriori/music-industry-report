# clipping. and Run the Jewels — Research for Report 7 Edits

## clipping.
- Noise rap group: Daveed Diggs, Jonathan Snipes, William Hutson
- On Sub Pop Records (indie rock label — Nirvana, Soundgarden)
- Diggs won Tony Award (Hamilton, 2016) and shared Grammy — mainstream fame that hasn't translated to hip-hop recognition
- Diggs: "being in Hamilton has made clipping okay, like it gives it a pass"
- *Splendor & Misery* (2016): Hugo Award nomination — first music album nominated since 1971
- "The Deep" received second Hugo nomination; spawned novella winning Lambda Literary Award
- More recognized by Hugo Awards than Grammys or BET Hip Hop Awards
- *Dead Channel Sky* (2025): Metacritic 82/100
- Group insists they are "a rap group" — not industrial or noise, in tradition of Dr. Dre and Bomb Squad
- Diggs writes exclusively in second or third person; no first-person language
- Being on indie rock label means marketed through indie rock channels, not hip-hop media pipelines

## Run the Jewels
- El-P: Company Flow (1992), *Funcrusher Plus* (1997), Definitive Jux label (artists kept masters, 50% profits)
- Killer Mike: OutKast associate, Grammy winner, "pledged allegiance to hip-hop independence" after major label frustrations
- All 4 RTJ albums released as free digital downloads
- RTJ2 (2014): 150,000 downloads in first 12 hours, Pitchfork/Stereogum/Spin album of year
- RTJ4 (2020): Released early during George Floyd protests — "NPR: a speaker box for society"
- RTJ4 completely shut out of 2021 Grammy nominations despite being "unofficial soundtrack of BLM revolution"
- Killer Mike response: "Fux whoever ain't fuck with us"
- Killer Mike later swept rap Grammys 2024 solo (Best Rap Album, Song, Performance) — beating Drake, Travis Scott, Nas
- El-P: "We are the label"
- Killer Mike: "The motivation to me is to make money and not be dependent upon the shallow pool called the entertainment world"
- Partnered with Mass Appeal (Nas), later BMG — not pure DIY but maintained independence in key ways
