# Griselda Records Collective: Research Profile

## Overview

Griselda Records is an American independent hip-hop label/collective based in Buffalo, New York, founded in 2012. Named after drug lord Griselda Blanco. The collective is centered on a family unit: **Westside Gunn** (Alvin Lamar Worthy, b. July 27, 1982) is the founder and visionary; his **half-brother Conway the Machine** (Demond Price, b. February 16, 1982) and **cousin Benny the Butcher** (Jeremie Pennick) are the other two core pillars. **Mach-Hommy** is listed as a co-founder alongside Westside Gunn and Conway, though his relationship with the collective has been intermittent (see Section 4).

## 1. Westside Gunn

### Discography — Studio Albums
- **FLYGOD** (March 11, 2016) — Debut studio album. Features production from Daringer, The Alchemist, Statik Selektah, Roc Marciano, Apollo Brown, Camouflage Monk. Guests include Conway, Benny, Action Bronson, Danny Brown, Mach-Hommy, Your Old Droog, Skyzoo, Roc Marciano, DJ Q-Bert.
  - Placed **#176 on Rolling Stone's 200 Greatest Hip-Hop Albums of All Time** (June 2022).
  - RYM rating: **3.73** from 4,607 ratings.
- **Supreme Blientele** (June 22, 2018) — Second studio album. Released via Griselda/EMPIRE.
  - RYM rating: **3.64** from 3,838 ratings.
  - Billboard 200 chart position: not confirmed in available sources.
- **Pray for Paris** (April 17, 2020) — Third studio album. Released via Griselda/EMPIRE.
  - Debuted at **#67 on the Billboard 200**.
  - Produced by Daringer, Beat Butcha, The Alchemist, DJ Muggs, DJ Premier.
  - Guests: Benny, Conway, Joey Badass, Tyler the Creator, Freddie Gibbs, Roc Marciano, Wale, Boldy James.
  - Album cover designed by **Virgil Abloh**, using Caravaggio's *David with the Head of Goliath*.
  - RYM rating: **3.58** from 10,588 ratings.
  - Metacritic score: **Not available on Metacritic** (not listed as a scored album). Note: Pray for Paris was Westside Gunn's **first-ever charting album** (confirmed by Rolling Stone).
- **And Then You Pray for Me** (October 13, 2023) — Fifth studio album. Released via Griselda/EMPIRE.
  - Debuted at **#29 on the Billboard 200** — his highest chart debut.
  - **20,000 equivalent album units** in first week (Luminate).
  - Hit **#1 on Billboard Emerging Artists chart** (week of Oct. 28, 2023).

### Hitler Wears Hermes Mixtape Series
A play on *The Devil Wears Prada*. The series spans **at least 12 installments** (as of 2024):
- Hitler Wears Hermes I (2012)
- Hitler Wears Hermes II (2014)
- Hitler Wears Hermes III (2015)
- Hitler Wears Hermes IV (2016)
- Hitler Wears Hermes V (2018)
- Hitler Wears Hermes VI (2018)
- Hitler Wears Hermes 7 (November 1, 2019)
- Hitler Wears Hermes 8: Side A (2021)
- Hitler Wears Hermes 8: Side B (2021)

- Hitler Wears Hermes 12 (2024, released after delays)

**Note:** Sources vary on whether the count is 10, 11, or 12 volumes due to HWH8 being split into multiple parts. The 12th installment is confirmed by UndergroundHipHopBlog.

### Art-World Crossover
- **Virgil Abloh relationship**: Abloh invited Gunn to Paris; they first met in person at the Off-White show (January 18, 2020). Abloh played Westside Gunn's music at the show, dressed him for fashion week, and inspired the *Pray for Paris* album. Abloh designed **four album covers** for Gunn before his death in November 2021.
- **Art Basel debut** (December 2021): Exhibited at the Diesel showroom in Miami's Wynwood neighborhood — his first-ever art exhibit, called "Vivian at the Art Basel." Featured paintings of DJ Khaled, Trick Daddy, Uncle Luke, Young Dolph, LeBron James.
- **Buffalo Kids**: Retail store opened March 6, 2021 at Walden Galleria in Cheektowaga, NY. Hybrid lifestyle clothing store and hip-hop art gallery. Houses brands: **Fashion Rebels** (est. 2014), **Fourth Rope** (wrestling-inspired brand, est. 2019), and Griselda by Fashion Rebels merchandise.
- **Roc Nation management deal** signed alongside the Shady Records deal era.

### Shady Records Timeline
- Signed to Shady Records (Eminem's label) in **2017**.
- Fulfilled contractual obligations and **departed Shady Records in November 2020** (announced on the Joe Budden Podcast).

## 2. Conway the Machine

### Background
- Real name: **Demond Price**, born February 16, 1982.
- In **2012**, Conway was **shot in the head, neck, and shoulder**. Doctors initially believed he would be paralyzed from the neck down. The only permanent injury is **partial facial paralysis (Bell's palsy)** on the right side of his face. He had to relearn how to eat and talk; recovery took approximately 1.5 years.
- Quote on the mental toll: *"When you gotta look at yourself in the mirror and you know that you don't look the same or your kids gotta see you don't look the same... it definitely takes a toll and it's like a war in your mind."* (Complex interview)

### Key Albums
- **Reject 2** (2015) — Early mixtape, established his reputation.
- **From King to a GOD** (2020) — Features production from Beat Butcha, Daringer, and others.
- **God Don't Make Mistakes** (February 25, 2022) — Released through Drumwork/Griselda/Shady/Interscope.
  - His **Shady Records debut** album.
  - **Metacritic score: 81** (based on 8 reviews).
  - Billboard 200 position: Sources indicate **#175** (single-source from Grokipedia; flag as needing verification).
  - Described as featuring "a collage of boom-bap that is as haunting as it is ornate."

### Shady Records Deal & Departure
- Conway signed to Shady Records as part of the Griselda collective deal.
- **Days before** the release of *God Don't Make Mistakes* (late February 2022), Conway publicly announced he had **departed from both Shady Records and Griselda Records**.
- On the Bootleg Kev Podcast: *"Like, sitting right here right now, I'm not on a contract with Griselda, Shady — none of that shit no more. It's free agency right now you heard."*
- Compared himself to Giannis Antetokounmpo: *"I need that supermax man, I need that Giannis bag."*
- On The Breakfast Club, regarding his contract: *"I didn't even read that contract, bro. I didn't read that shit. I just signed that shit and moved on. Unfortunately, the contract wasn't in my favor."* When asked if he got money for signing, he said he got *"some pocket change"* across three contracts (Shady, Interscope, Griselda).
- **Later expressed regret** about the public comments: told interviewers that Westside Gunn *"didn't deserve that"* and his statements were *"blown out of proportion."*
- Founded his own label, **Drumwork Music Group**.

## 3. Benny the Butcher

### Background
- Real name: **Jeremie Pennick**.
- Cousin of Westside Gunn.
- His older brother **Machine Gun Black (Marchello Lowery)** was shot and killed in a drive-by on Montana Block, Buffalo, on October 3, 2006.

### Key Albums
- **Tana Talk 3** (2018) — Breakthrough project.
- **The Plugs I Met** (2019) — Produced by Harry Fraud.
- **Burden of Proof** (October 16, 2020) — Released via Griselda/EMPIRE.
  - Produced **entirely by Hit-Boy**.
  - Features: Rick Ross, Lil Wayne, Freddie Gibbs, Big Sean, Conway, Westside Gunn, Dom Kennedy, Queen Naija.
  - **Metacritic score: 82** (based on 6 reviews).
  - Debuted at **#27 on the Billboard 200** — the **highest-charting Griselda project** at the time.
  - **19,000 first-week units** (14,190 streaming equivalent, 4,226 pure sales, 309 track equivalent).
- **Tana Talk 4** (March 11, 2022) — Released via Griselda/Black Soprano Family/EMPIRE.
  - Features: J. Cole, Diddy, Conway, Westside Gunn, Boldy James, Stove God Cooks, 38 Spesh.
  - Debuted at **#22 on the Billboard 200** — his highest-charting project.
  - **20,000 first-week equivalent album units** (6,000 pure sales).
  - Metacritic score: page exists but exact aggregate score not confirmed from search results. (Flag: needs direct verification.)
- **Everybody Can't Go** (January 26, 2024) — His **Def Jam major label debut**.

### 2020 Shooting
- On **November 14, 2020**, Benny was **shot in the leg** during an armed robbery in a **Walmart parking lot on S. Rice Avenue in southwest Houston, Texas** at approximately 4:30 PM.
- He was getting out of his Rolls Royce when a group of men demanded his chains and necklaces. When he moved too slowly, one suspect fired, hitting him in the leg.
- He was hospitalized and **discharged the same weekend**. Two others with him were uninjured. Suspects fled in a Chevy Malibu.
- Widely covered: ABC13, KHOU, CBS Texas, Washington Post, Revolt, Hypebeast.

### Deal History
- Originally signed to Griselda Records.
- **April 2017**: Benny and Black Soprano Family (BSF) signed a label deal with **Entertainment One (eOne) Music**.
- **April 2020**: Expanded global label deal between eOne and BSF.
- **November 12, 2021**: Signed to **Def Jam Recordings**. Announced by **Snoop Dogg** on Joe Rogan's podcast. Snoop (then serving as Def Jam's executive creative consultant) intervened when Benny said the label initially lowballed him: *"I'm going to call the boss"* — Snoop ensured Benny received a higher deal.
- Major label debut album *Everybody Can't Go* released January 2024 on Def Jam.

## 4. Mach-Hommy

### Background
- Haitian-American rapper. Identity and real name kept deliberately mysterious — he rarely shows his face and gives few interviews.
- **Co-founded Griselda Records** in 2012 alongside Westside Gunn and Conway the Machine (per Wikipedia and multiple sources).

### Griselda Relationship
- His first run with Griselda was **short-lived**. A feud erupted between Westside Gunn and Griselda affiliate **Tha God Fahim**. Mach-Hommy sided with Fahim, and the two formed the **Dump Gawd** collective.
- The feud ran from approximately **2017-2019**.
- **Reconciliation**: On Christmas Day 2020, Gunn posted a photo of himself with Mach-Hommy and Tha God Fahim together for the first time in years.
- Westside Gunn then **executive produced** *Pray for Haiti* (2021), solidifying the reconciliation.
- **Key distinction**: Mach-Hommy is a co-founder of Griselda but his relationship has been fluid — he has appeared on Griselda releases but operates with significant independence.

### Pray for Haiti (May 21, 2021)
- Released through **Griselda Records and Daupe!**
- Executive produced by **Westside Gunn and Mach-Hommy**.
- Production by: **Camouflage Monk, Conductor Williams, Denny LaFlare, Cee Gee, DJ Green Lantern, Messiah Muzik, Nicholas Craven, Sadhu Gold**.
- Guests: Westside Gunn, Keisha Plum, Melanie Charles, Tha God Fahim.
- **Metacritic score: 85** (based on 8 reviews) — **the highest Metacritic score of any Griselda-affiliated release**.
- Mach-Hommy announced **20% of profits** donated to the Pray for Haiti Trust Fund for educational infrastructure in Haiti.

### Pricing Model
- Mach-Hommy is notorious for extreme pricing on physical releases:
  - **Cassettes: ~$70**
  - **CDs: ~$100**
  - **Vinyl: no lower than $115, as high as $5,000+**
- This model deliberately devalues streaming revenue in favor of scarcity-driven physical sales. With this approach, streaming platforms become promotional tools rather than revenue centers.
- *Pray for Haiti* on Daupe!/Griselda: pink cassette limited to **only 80 copies**.

## 6. In-House Production: Daringer, Beat Butcha, Conductor Williams

### Daringer (Thomas Paladino)
- **In-house producer** for Griselda since its founding. Has been producing since 2005.
- Defined the **Griselda sound**: dark, gritty, 1990s-inspired boom-bap. Cinematic samples chopped from soul, funk, and film scores over hard-hitting drums.
- Quote on creating the sound: Daringer has explained that the sonic identity developed organically from his partnership with Westside Gunn.
- Key credits: FLYGOD, Supreme Blientele, Pray for Paris, WWCD, virtually all early Griselda releases.

### Beat Butcha
- Known for **dissonant, modern take on '90s boom-bap**.
- Co-produced **WWCD** (2019) entirely with Daringer — notably **without any samples**, a significant logistical and creative challenge given Griselda's sample-heavy reputation.
- Also produced on Conway's *From King to a GOD*.

### Conductor Williams (Denzel Williams)
- Frequent collaborator, **not an in-house Griselda producer** but deeply associated.
- Style: **loop-heavy, vinyl-esque sample chops** with gritty boom-bap drums.
- Has produced for Westside Gunn, Conway, Boldy James, Rome Streetz, and also for Drake, J. Cole, Tyler the Creator.
- Collaborated with Rome Streetz on *Trainspotting* (2025).
- NPR described the Westside Gunn/Conductor Williams partnership as *"perfecting rap's most refined aesthetic."*

### The Production-Independence Connection
Having in-house production (Daringer, Beat Butcha) means Griselda controls its sonic identity without relying on expensive outside producers or label-mandated sounds. This is a key pillar of their independence — they can release music at an extraordinary pace with a consistent sound, keeping costs low and creative control total.

## 7. The Collective Model

### Structure
- **Griselda Records** functions as both an independent label and a loose collective.
- Westside Gunn is the **founder and primary executive** — he has described himself as the visionary and architect.
- The label has operated on a model where core members can pursue outside deals while remaining affiliated. This mirrors the **Wu-Tang Clan model**: collective identity with individual solo careers.
  - Conway signed to Shady/Interscope while on Griselda, then departed both.
  - Benny signed to Def Jam while still affiliated with Griselda/BSF.
  - Boldy James signed to Griselda for specific projects.

### Wu-Tang Comparison (Their Own Words)
- **Westside Gunn**: *"That Wu-Tang cloth? We're cut from it. Mobb Deep, Nas, all them people... Griselda could have came out in the '90s."* (NPR, 2019)
- **Conway the Machine**: *"We ain't really seen nothing like that since the Clan."* (referring to their vinyl selling out in minutes)
- The comparison is apt in structure (family-based collective, solo careers, in-house production identity) but differs in scale and era.

### Revenue Model
- **Limited-run vinyl**: Partnership with **Daupe! Media** for exclusive vinyl pressings that sell out in minutes and are never repressed. Creates artificial scarcity and collector's market value.
- **Merchandise**: Limited quantities of hoodies, t-shirts with unique designs through Fashion Rebels and Griselda branding.
- **Art prints and gallery**: Westside Gunn's Buffalo Kids store and Art Basel presence.
- **Touring/shows**: Standard concert revenue.
- **Distribution through EMPIRE**: Maintains independence while accessing major distribution infrastructure.
- **No radio dependence**: Westside Gunn — *"We have the largest body of work in the shortest amount of time... without one song on radio."*
- **Benny the Butcher on independence**: *"It's the Griselda movement. We got to cut a check to somebody? For what?"*

## 10. The Buffalo Connection

### Geographic Positioning
- Buffalo, NY is **not** one of hip-hop's traditional power centers (NYC, LA, Atlanta, Houston, Chicago). Before Griselda, Buffalo had essentially **no presence on the rap map**.
- Westside Gunn: *"This was the first time people ever really heard Buffalo before."*
- Buffalo is described as *"very, very poor"* — the economic conditions of the city directly inform the music's content (drug dealing, violence, economic desperation).
- The isolation worked **both for and against** them: against because they lacked industry infrastructure and connections; for because it forced radical independence and created an authentic outsider identity that resonated with listeners tired of industry-manufactured artists.

### Cultural Impact on Buffalo
- Griselda **fundamentally changed Buffalo's cultural identity** in the hip-hop world.
- Westside Gunn opened the **Buffalo Kids** store — described as a *"huge step forward in honoring Buffalo's own."*
- The city's hip-hop ecosystem has grown from *"fickle, underserved"* to *"a thriving, diverse community of rappers, singers, and producers."*
- Griselda appeared on **The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon** (covered by local Buffalo NBC affiliate WGRZ).

### Industry Co-Signs from Buffalo Era
- Publicly supported by **Raekwon** (Wu-Tang Clan), **Busta Rhymes**, and **Nas**.
- Signed to **Eminem's Shady Records** (2017).
- Signed management deal with **Jay-Z's Roc Nation**.
