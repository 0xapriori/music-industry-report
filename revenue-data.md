# US Recorded Music Revenue Data

All figures at estimated retail value unless otherwise noted. Primary source: RIAA year-end reports.

## Annual Revenue (1999–2024)

| Year | Revenue (Billions) | YoY Change | Notes |
|------|-------------------|------------|-------|
| 1999 | ~$14.6B | — | **Peak year (nominal)**. ~$22–24B inflation-adjusted |
| 2000 | ~$14.3B | -2% | CD shipments peaked at $13.2B |
| 2001 | ~$13.7B | -4% | Post-Napster decline begins |
| 2002 | ~$12.6B | -8% | P2P file sharing accelerates |
| 2003 | ~$11.9B | -6% | iTunes launches (Apr 28); 17.4% cumulative decline from 2000 |
| 2004 | ~$12.2B | +3% | Slight rebound |
| 2005 | ~$12.3B | +1% | `[single-source estimate]` |
| 2006 | ~$11.8B | -4% | |
| 2007 | ~$10.4B | -12% | Accelerating decline |
| 2008 | ~$8.5–9.0B | -14 to -18% | Financial crisis compounds digital disruption |
| 2009 | ~$6.3–7.8B | varies | *Sources disagree — range reflects different methodologies* |
| 2010 | ~$7.0B | — | Near the **trough** |
| 2011 | ~$7.1B | +1% | Spotify launches in US (July) |
| 2012 | ~$7.1B | flat | Digital download peak |
| 2013 | ~$7.0B | -1% | Streaming emerging |
| 2014 | ~$6.97B | flat | **Trough** of the decline |
| 2015 | ~$7.0B | flat | Apple Music launches; recovery begins |
| 2016 | ~$7.7B | +11.4% | Streaming-driven growth |
| 2017 | ~$8.7B | +13% | |
| 2018 | ~$9.8B | +12% | Third straight year of double-digit growth |
| 2019 | ~$11.1B | +13% | |
| 2020 | ~$12.2B | +9.2% | Growth despite COVID |
| 2021 | ~$15.0B | +23% | Surpasses 1999 nominal peak |
| 2022 | ~$15.9B | +6.1% | |
| 2023 | ~$17.1B | +8% | |
| 2024 | ~$17.7B | +3% | 100M paid streaming subscribers |

**Note on 2025 forward**: Starting in 2025, the RIAA switched to **wholesale** reporting (aligning with IFPI standards). The 2024 figure of $17.7B retail = $11.3B wholesale. Direct comparison with prior years' retail figures requires adjustment.

*Sources: RIAA year-end reports (2018, 2020, 2022, 2024), Music Business Research, Billboard, Digital Music News, Variety*

## Data Confidence Notes

- **1999–2003**: Figures widely cited across multiple secondary sources referencing RIAA data. High confidence.
- **2004–2009**: Some individual year figures harder to pin down from multiple independent sources. The 2009 figure shows the most variance ($6.3B–$7.8B) — likely due to different calculation methods (wholesale vs. retail) or scope definitions.
- **2010–2015**: Moderate confidence; transition period with mixed format reporting.
- **2016–2024**: High confidence; sourced directly from RIAA published reports available online.
